The data engineer dilemma

Your organization is growing. You've accumulated data across different systems and you want to become more data-driven. The natural instinct? Hire a data engineer.

But at 100K per year, is that really the best investment?

We don't think so. Here's why.

Data engineers don't add business value

That sounds provocative, but hear us out. Data engineers are essential for building and maintaining data infrastructure. They set up pipelines, manage data quality, and ensure data flows from A to B.

But here's the thing: moving data doesn't create insights. Data engineers lay the foundation, but they don't directly answer business questions or drive decisions.

It's your data analysts who turn data into insights. They analyze trends, build dashboards, identify opportunities, and help you make better decisions. That's where the real value is.
